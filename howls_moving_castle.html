Howl's Moving Castle (ハウルの動く城, Hauru no Ugoku Shiro) is a 2004 Japanese animated fantasy film written and directed by Hayao Miyazaki, loosely based on the 1986 novel by British author Diana Wynne Jones.

Influenced by Miyazaki's opposition to the 2003 Iraq War, the film contains strong anti-war themes. It also explores ideas of aging, compassion, and feminism, portraying old age as a source of freedom rather than weakness.

The film premiered at the 61st Venice International Film Festival and was released in Japan on 20 November 2004. It grossed over $236 million worldwide and received an Academy Award nomination for Best Animated Feature.
